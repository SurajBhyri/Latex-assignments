\item  Find the ratio in which the line segment joining the points (-3, 10) and (6, â€“ 8) is divided by (-1, 6).  \\
\solution \\
Given Data:\\
           A = \myvec{-3\\10}\\
           B = \myvec{6\\-8}\\
           C = \myvec{-1\\6}\\
           \includegraphics{/home/clab18/Linear-equation/10.7.2.4/figs/Figure_1.png}
To find: ratio dividing them\\
Now, 
\begin{align}
C = \frac{A+nB}{n+1}\\
C = \frac{1}{1+n} \times \myvec{\myvec{-3\\10}+n\times \myvec{6\\-8}}\\
= \frac{1}{1+n} \times \myvec{-3+6n\\10-8n}
\end{align}
By taking x
\begin{align}
-1= \frac{-3+6n}{1+n}\\
\implies -3+6n=-1-n\\
\implies 6n+n =-1+3\\
 \implies 7n=2\\
 \implies n=\frac{2}{7}
\end{align}	
now, by taking y 
\begin{align}
6= \frac{10-8n}{n+1}\\
\implies 10-8n=6+6n\\
\implies 10-6=6n+8n\\
\implies 4=14n\\
\implies n=\frac{2}{7}
\end{align}
Therefore,the ratio which divides A and B is 2:7.\\
\end{enumerate}
